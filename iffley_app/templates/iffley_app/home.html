Welcome to the Iffley Web Guide

A digital collection of the routes from Iffley Road Climbing Wall, with grades, holds, and pictures to help climbers use the wall.

Rules
  1. Most routes are sit starts. For these routes, any hold you can reach while sat on the floor is an allowed starting hold.
  2. For stand start routes, the holds for the standing start are in ( brackets ) and are highlighted in green, you must only use these holds to start.
  3. The finish hold is the final hold listed, it is highlighted in red.
  4. You do not need to match the final hold, one hand is enough.
  5. Chips are always allowed for feet, but only allowed as hands for problems 5c and harder unless otherwise specified.
Grades

A variety of different grading systems have been used at Iffley, of varying helpfulness and quality. The most commonly used grade is the Iffley Tech Grade. These are loosely based on British tech grades but do not track them precisely, a rough visual guide is included below.



For beginners to the wall I would suggest starting out in the 4s to get accustomed to the wall and the holds. 5a through 5c can be considered intermediate problems and 6s are generally considered hard.

The other main type of grade used at Iffley is the B grade. These work much like the adjectival grades in UK trad climbing (e.g HVS, E1, VDiff) and give an overall impression as to how hard the problem is, accounting for technical difficulty, length, and risk of injury.
